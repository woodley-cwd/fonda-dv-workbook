## Thumbnail Style

Use high-contrast, emotionally charged thumbnails inspired by Mel Robbins, Myron Golden, and The Diary of a CEO. The best-performing look for this niche is bold, simple, and curiosity-driven: one strong subject, a clean background, large readable text, and a clear emotional reaction or tension point.

Colors

Add bold, saturated accent colors like yellow, red, white, or electric blue when you need urgency or curiosity, which fits the more direct, high-energy Myron Golden approach. For Mel Robbins-style thumbnails, keep the palette polished and modern, with clean neutrals and one strong accent color. Primary colors

Composition
Prioritize a large face or central subject, tight crop, and minimal clutter. Use asymmetrical layouts when possible, with the subject on one side and big text or a visual hook on the other, so the thumbnail creates instant tension or curiosity. Keep the composition simple enough to read on mobile, with only one main idea per thumbnail.

Style
Lean into a podcast-meets-coaching aesthetic: cinematic but clean, professional but personal, and designed to feel smart and clickable. Use expressive facial emotion, dramatic lighting, and short punchy text that creates contrast, conflict, or curiosity. The overall vibe should feel premium, authoritative, and motivational, not busy or overly designed.
